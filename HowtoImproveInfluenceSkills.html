Five Key Points to Keep in Mind

In our training, we often stress the importance of the following five significant pieces of advice. Following this counsel, you can ensure the stable growth of your Influence skills.

  1. Think about your audience

Wherever you communicate with people - at negotiations, at a conference, in a narrow circle of colleagues, at an executive meeting, one-on-one - it is imperative to think about the interlocutor and speak in a language they would understand. Does the person understand the topic, what are their views and values, how to explain the nuances to them, how to attract their attention, and how to provide them with useful information?

  1. Thoroughly prepare for any talks, however menial

It would help if you took notes and plan. Of course, improvisation can bear great results. Nevertheless, you must prepare a solid base even if you are going to improvise by collecting and analyzing the information, feeling the topic, and studying your audience's interests.

  1. Listen carefully

Oddly enough, the dialogue sometimes resembles two monologues - each speaker pushes their point, disregarding the opponent's opinions. Not everyone is ready to listen to their interlocutor, to be sensitive and attentive. And without this, no successful communication is possible.

Listening attentively, delving into a person's words, trying to understand them is not so easy! But at our training, we teach empathic listening, which your interlocutors will appreciate. And you will increase the chances of successful negotiations.

  1. Clarify unclear statements by rephrasing

It is essential to rephrase the conversation when appropriate. "Did I understand you correctly that ..?" These are the right questions to avoid misunderstandings. You also show that you respect the other person's words.

  1. Practice your influence skills by speaking in public

Skills of speaking in front of a broad audience, at a conference, conducting discussions with colleagues will come in handy for a career boost. Capturing the audience's attention, being an exciting storyteller, influencing listeners, defending one's position, and changing the audience's mood are the critical skills of a leader, anyone who wants to achieve success in a business environment. We teach these irreplaceable skills in our training.
